Guide

Understanding Gold Pricing

What is the "spot price"?

The spot price is the current market price for one troy ounce of pure (999) gold, set by global trading on exchanges like COMEX and the London Bullion Market. It's the reference price this tool converts into grams, tolas, and kilograms across different purities and currencies.

Karat purity explained

Pure gold (24K) is soft, so it's alloyed with metals like copper and silver for durability. The karat number tells you how many parts out of 24 are gold:

Grams, tola, and troy ounce

Different regions use different weight units for gold. A tola (≈11.6638 g) is the standard unit across Pakistan, India, and the wider South Asian and Gulf markets. A troy ounce (≈31.1035 g) is the international standard used for spot pricing worldwide.

What affects the price you pay

  • Spot price: the base global market rate, driven by supply, demand, and currency strength.
  • Making charges: labor cost added by jewelers for crafting jewelry, usually a percentage or flat fee.
  • Import duties & taxes: vary significantly by country and affect the final retail rate.
  • Purity: lower karat gold costs less per gram than 24K, since it contains less actual gold.

Smart Buying Tips

Always ask for a hallmark or purity certificate before buying jewelry.
Compare the spot price shown here against your dealer's quoted rate to spot excessive markups.
Making charges are negotiable in most markets — don't hesitate to ask.
For investment, 24K bars or coins carry the least premium over spot price.
Gold prices can move throughout the trading day — check before large purchases.

Frequently Asked Questions

How often does the gold price update?+
This tool refreshes live spot prices automatically about every 45 seconds using a real-time gold price feed, and you can also refresh manually at any time.
What's the difference between 24K, 22K, 21K, and 18K gold?+
The karat number indicates gold purity out of 24 parts. 24K is 99.9% pure gold, 22K is about 91.6% pure, 21K is about 87.5% pure, and 18K is 75% pure, with the remainder made up of other metals for durability.
What is a tola?+
A tola is a traditional South Asian unit of mass equal to approximately 11.6638 grams, commonly used for pricing gold in Pakistan, India, and neighboring countries.
Why does gold price differ by country?+
The global spot price of gold is the same, but local prices vary due to currency exchange rates, import duties, taxes, and making charges added by local jewelers and dealers.
Is this gold price checker accurate?+
Yes. Prices are pulled from a live spot price feed sourced from real market data providers, converted using live currency exchange rates. Local retail prices may vary slightly due to dealer markups.
