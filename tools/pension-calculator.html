Pension Guide

How to Use This Pension Calculator

Planning for retirement starts with understanding how much income you can expect once you stop working. This pension calculator gives you a quick way to estimate your monthly pension, gratuity and commutation amount using the figures common to most defined-benefit pension schemes.

Enter your last drawn basic salary, total years of service, and the pension rate used by your employer or scheme. The calculator multiplies these figures together to estimate your gross monthly pension, then applies your gratuity rate and commutation settings to show a fuller picture of your retirement benefits.

How Is Monthly Pension Calculated?

Most pension schemes calculate monthly pension as your last drawn salary multiplied by your years of service and a pension rate, then divided by 100. Rates and formulas vary widely between government schemes, private employers and countries, so treat the result as a starting estimate rather than an official figure.

What Is Gratuity?

Gratuity is typically a one-time lump sum paid at retirement, calculated from your last drawn salary, years of service and a gratuity rate set by your employer or scheme rules. It is meant to reward long service and provide immediate funds at the start of retirement.

What Is Pension Commutation?

Commutation allows a retiree to convert part of their future monthly pension into an immediate lump sum. In exchange, the monthly pension is permanently reduced by the commuted percentage. The purchase-years figure reflects how many years of pension value are used to calculate the lump sum, which is common in many government pension systems.

Why Estimate Your Pension Early?

Knowing an approximate pension figure years before retirement helps with budgeting, savings decisions and other financial planning. Adjusting the inputs in this calculator lets you see how a raise, a promotion or extra years of service could change your eventual retirement income.
