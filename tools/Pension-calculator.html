How It Works

Pension Formulas Explained

Understand the exact calculations used behind the scenes for each pension type.

Government Pension

Pension = Last Pay × Service × 7/300

Monthly pension is based on your last drawn basic pay, multiplied by total years of service and a fixed multiplier fraction set by government pension rules.

Private Sector Pension

Pension = Salary × Service × Rate%

Company pension schemes typically apply a percentage multiplier (often 1.5%–2.5%) to your last salary for every year of service completed.

EOBI Pension

Pension = Avg Wage × Years × 2%

EOBI pension uses your average contributory wage multiplied by insurable years of service and the EOBI accrual rate, subject to the minimum pension guarantee.

Gratuity

Gratuity = Last Pay × Service × Rate

A one-time lump sum paid at retirement, calculated using your last basic pay, total service years, and the gratuity rate specified by your organization.

Commutation

Lump Sum = Commuted Pension × Factor × 12

You can commute up to 35% of your monthly pension in exchange for an upfront lump sum, calculated with an age-based commutation factor table.

Annual Increase Projection

Future Pension = Pension × (1 + Rate)ⁿ

Projects your monthly pension over future years if the government announces a fixed annual percentage increase, compounded year over year.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about pension calculation.

How is pension calculated?+
Pension is generally calculated using your last drawn basic salary, total years of service, and a pension multiplier fraction (commonly 7/300 or 1/60 depending on the scheme). The formula is: Last Pay Drawn × Years of Service × Multiplier Fraction.
What is commutation in pension?+
Commutation is a lump sum amount you receive upfront by giving up a portion (commonly up to 35%) of your monthly pension for a fixed number of years, calculated using an age-based commutation factor table.
What is gratuity and how is it different from pension?+
Gratuity is a one-time lump sum payment made at retirement based on your last salary and years of service, while pension is a recurring monthly payment for life. Some employees receive both, others choose one.
Does this pension calculator work for EOBI?+
Yes, this calculator includes a dedicated EOBI mode that estimates pension based on average contributory wages and insurable years of service using the EOBI minimum pension rules.
Is this pension calculator accurate for official use?+
This tool gives a close estimate based on common pension formulas. Actual pension amounts are finalized by your employer's pension department or EOBI office and may vary based on specific rules, revisions, and increases.
Can I change the pension multiplier formula?+
Yes, under Government mode you can select from standard formulas like 7/300, 1/60, 1/70, or enter your own custom numerator and denominator if your department uses a different fraction.
Does the calculator store or send my salary data anywhere?+
No. All calculations run directly in your browser using JavaScript. Your salary and service details are never uploaded, stored, or shared with any server.
What is the minimum pension in Pakistan?+
The government and EOBI periodically notify a minimum pension amount that every eligible pensioner must receive regardless of the calculated formula result. This calculator lets you enter the current notified minimum for EOBI mode.
