How to Merge PDF Files Online — Step by Step

Combining multiple PDF documents into one file is one of the most common document tasks, whether you're assembling a job application, compiling monthly invoices, or organizing scanned pages into a single report. This tool merges files entirely inside your browser, so nothing is uploaded to a server at any point.

  • Step 1: Drag your PDF files into the box above, or click to browse and select them (2 or more files required).
  • Step 2: Drag the file cards — or use the arrow buttons — to set the exact order pages should appear in the final document.
  • Step 3: Optionally, enter a page range for any file (e.g. 1-3,5) if you only want specific pages from it.
  • Step 4: Name your output file and click Merge PDFs.
  • Step 5: Download the combined PDF — it's generated instantly, with no watermark added.

Common reasons to merge PDF files

  • Job applications: Combine your resume, cover letter, and certificates into a single PDF before submitting.
  • Invoices & receipts: Group related billing documents into one file for accounting or expense claims.
  • Reports & proposals: Merge chapters, appendices, or contributions from multiple team members into a final document.
  • Scanned paperwork: Turn individually scanned pages into one organized, shareable PDF.

Why merge PDFs in your browser instead of uploading them?

Many free online PDF mergers work by uploading your files to a remote server, processing them there, and then offering a download link — which means your documents briefly exist on someone else's infrastructure. This tool takes a different approach: it uses a JavaScript PDF library running directly in your browser tab, so your files never leave your device. That makes it a better fit for anything containing personal, financial, or confidential information.

Tips for a clean merged PDF

  • Rename files before uploading (e.g. "01-cover.pdf", "02-resume.pdf") to make ordering easier at a glance.
  • Use the page range field to skip blank or duplicate pages instead of merging entire files.
  • Double-check page counts in the file list — the total shown updates automatically as you add or remove files.
  • Keep a copy of your original files until you've confirmed the merged PDF opens correctly.
